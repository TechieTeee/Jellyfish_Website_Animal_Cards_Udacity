Jellyfish

Jellyfish are the immortal creatures of the sea with a unique ability to revert to their larva form after reaching adulthood.

  • Scientific Name: Subphylum Medusozoa
  • Size Range: 0.5 Inches to 16 Inches in Length :
  • Average Lifespan: : Immortal
  • Habitat: Oceans- Usually In Coastal Areas

Jellyfish are known for their vibrant, neon colors and translucent bodies. They are some of the most awe-inspiring creatures of the sea. There are more than 2,000 verified species in the world.While they are enticingly beautiful, many varieties they are extremely dangerous. These varieties excrete toxins upon being touched as a defense mechanism, and these toxins can be deadly to other animals and humans alike. Also, while domesticated jellyfish can live 2 -4 years on average, jellyfish in the wild can live much longer because of their ability to revert to larva and reproduce asexually. This makes it challenging to determine the exact age of the longest living jellyfish, and many scientists have deemed them "immortal".
